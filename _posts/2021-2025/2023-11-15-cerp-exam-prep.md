In October 2023, I successfully completed the CERP examination. Below is a summary of my preparation efforts with links to resources I used and created. Hopefully, this information will be helpful to anyone currently planning to take the exam. 

## ABA's CERP Exam Online Prep Course
After having such an excellent experience with the ABA's CRCM prep course in 2022, I didn’t hesitate to purchase the [ABA's CERP Exam Online Prep course]https://www.aba.com/training-events/online-training/cerp-exam-online-prep). Unfortunately, this course was a significant disappointment. Unlike the CRCM course, which felt cohesive, this program used a rotating cast of instructors for different risk domains. The modules covering financial risk were particularly difficult to follow and lacked engagement. Overall, the structure felt disorganized for the $1,045 price tag. The course did include a practice exam, which was the most valuable component for gauging my readiness.

## Study Notes
As I did when preparing for the CRCM exam, I created my own study notes. The process of creating and organizing my notes was a more effective study method than the online course. You can view some of my [CERP study notes here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1c8mnEtobFvOnN4fg867_rsTKHei8lFnZTRhiAlv5R3s/edit?usp=sharing){:target="_blank"}.

## Flash Cards
I relied heavily on a large stack of flashcards to internalize key terms, risk frameworks, and calculation rules. This method remains my go-to for drilling technical information until it becomes second nature.

## The Exam
I sat for the exam at a proctored testing center. The security protocol is standard: you’ll be required to store all personal items (keys, phone, wallet) in a secure locker before entering the testing room. Much like the CRCM, the computer-based format provides your pass/fail status immediately upon completion.

## Takeaway
If you are deciding whether to spend the money on the official prep course, I would suggest looking into the [Comptroller's Handbooks](https://www.occ.treas.gov/publications-and-resources/publications/comptrollers-handbook/index-comptrollers-handbook.html){:target="_blank"} as a free supplement to help bridge the gaps in liquidity and market risk, which I found to be the weakest parts of the ABA’s CERP curriculum.
